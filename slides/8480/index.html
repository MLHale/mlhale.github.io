First Principle

"the first basis from which a thing is known"

- Aristotle

 

a building block of reasoning that sits at the bottom of a stack of subsequent rationale. It is a self-evident proposition or idea that cannot be fundamentally broken down by other other propositions or ideas.

First Principle

We learn first principles by induction (ἐπαγωγή), a form of cognitive development that begins with perception and progresses through a series of increasingly sophisticated states in which various universal concepts come to be formed in our souls

Question 3: How can I Better connect First Principles throughout my PRe/camp/Post-camp curricula?

slides.mlhale.com/gencyber2021/

REtrofit

If you already have curricular content, go through it and identify points in the instructional design where first principles can be reinforced.

If you can't find first principles in your lessons, ask yourself if the lesson is really valuable or if the instructional design/learning objectives are the right ones?

Design To

If you are starting fresh, design your lessons around reinforcing certain principles. Ask yourself if the examples in your lessons are the best ones? Do they provide conceptual clarity? Do they generalize?

 

Design reinforcement moments in active learning activities.
