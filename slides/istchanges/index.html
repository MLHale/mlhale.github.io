Constitutional Change Process Overview

  1. Amendment introduction
  2. Amendment presented as a motion by the IS&T Advisory Committee.  (Does not require a second as it comes from a committee.)
  3. Discussion of amendment (alternates between pro/con) until either:
    a) there is no further discussion
    b) a motion to end discussion is approved
    c) secondary motion to postpone discussion or refer the motion to a committee is approved
  4. Repeat step #1-3 for each amendment
  5. For each motion whose discussion ends with 3(a) or 3(b)
    a) Ballots will be distributed
    b) After 10 working days, ballot will be counted
    c) 2/3 majority required for approval
